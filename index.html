REU 2021: Thin Film Structures in Solar Panels

Technologies: Molecular Dynamics, LAMMPS, Nudged Elastic Band method

This project involved studying thin film structures, specifically focusing on the energy barriers of Selenium (Se) interstitials in Cadmium Telluride (CdTe) and Cadmium Selenide (CdSe) using molecular dynamics simulations. The goal was to understand how these structures impact the efficiency of solar cells.

  • Investigated atomic interactions and energy barriers in semiconductor materials.
  • Utilized computational models to simulate material properties and diffusion.

Numerical Simulation of Time-Resolved Photoluminescence for Solar Cells

Technologies: MatLab, COMSOL Multiphysics®

This project focused on using numerical simulations of Time-Resolved Photoluminescence (TRPL) to better understand recombination mechanisms in photovoltaic materials like Cd(Se, Te) solar cells. The research aimed to improve solar cell performance by analyzing TRPL data at various light intensities, material thicknesses, and charge carrier mobilities.

  • Prepared theoretical data of single photon time-resolved photoluminescence.
  • Conducted error analysis of theoretical data to experimental data using R-squared regression.
  • Explored the effectiveness of analytical models for TRPL data.
